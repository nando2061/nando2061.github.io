Merneith

Merneith was an early Egyptian ruler who lived during the 1st Dynasty (c. 3000-2890 BCE). She is considered one of the first women in recorded history to hold significant power, possibly even ruling as a pharaoh in her own right. Evidence from her tomb at Abydos suggests she had a status equivalent to that of a king, including a tomb structure and grave goods comparable to male rulers of the time. Merneith was likely the consort of King Djet and the mother of King Den. Her prominence is attributed to her role as regent for her young son after Djet's death. While her exact title and role remain debated, inscriptions and artifacts point to her significant influence in the political and religious life of early Egypt.
