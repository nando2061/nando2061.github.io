Alexander the great

Alexander the Great, while best known as the legendary king of Macedonia and one of history's most renowned conquerors, also held the title of Pharaoh of Egypt. In 332 BCE, during his campaign against the Persian Empire, Alexander captured Egypt, which was then under Persian control. He was welcomed as a liberator by the Egyptians, who resented Persian rule. In Memphis, Alexander was crowned Pharaoh and honored with traditional Egyptian ceremonies. During his time in Egypt, he founded the city of Alexandria, which would become a major cultural, intellectual, and economic hub of the ancient world. Alexander showed respect for Egyptian religion and culture, even consulting the Oracle of Amun at the Siwa Oasis, which confirmed his divine status in the eyes of the local population. His reign marked the beginning of a new era in Egyptian history, blending Greek and Egyptian influences that would define the Ptolemaic period following his death. Alexander's time in Egypt was brief but pivotal, leaving a legacy that shaped the region's identity for centuries.
