Kings

Shepseskaf

Shepseskaf was an ancient Egyptian pharaoh of the Fourth Dynasty during the Old Kingdom, reigning around 2500 BCE. He succeeded Menkaure and is known for deviating from the tradition of pyramid construction established by his predecessors. Instead of building a pyramid, Shepseskaf constructed a large rectangular tomb called the Mastabat al-Fir’aun (“Pharaoh's Bench”) in Saqqara, signaling a shift in royal burial practices. His reign, though relatively short, marked the end of the Fourth Dynasty's pyramid-building era, which was characterized by monumental projects like the Great Pyramid of Giza. Little is known about his political or economic achievements, but his choice of burial style has led scholars to speculate about possible religious or administrative changes during his time. He was succeeded by Userkaf, the founder of the Fifth Dynasty.
