Neferkara

Neferkara is a relatively obscure pharaoh of ancient Egypt's 2nd Dynasty, with limited historical evidence about his reign. His name, meaning "Beautiful is the Ka of Ra," appears only in later king lists, such as the Abydos King List, and not in contemporary inscriptions or artifacts. He is sometimes associated with the transitional period between the 2nd and 3rd Dynasties, though his exact position in the sequence of rulers is debated. Some Egyptologists theorize that Neferkara may have had a short reign or that he was a minor or ephemeral ruler. His historical significance remains uncertain due to the lack of archaeological evidence directly linked to his name.
