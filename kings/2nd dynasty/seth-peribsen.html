Seth-Peribsen

Seth-Peribsen was a ruler of ancient Egypt during the Second Dynasty, around the 27th century BCE. He is notable for being one of the few pharaohs to adopt the name "Seth," the Egyptian god of chaos and disorder, instead of the traditional Horus, which was associated with kingship and divine rule. This choice has led some scholars to speculate that Seth-Peribsen's reign might have been marked by political turmoil or a break from the established religious traditions. His reign is not well-documented, and archaeological evidence is limited, but he is believed to have ruled for a brief period before being succeeded by Khasekhemwy. Seth-Peribsen's tomb is located at Abydos, and his name is found on a few surviving artifacts, but his reign remains somewhat enigmatic in the context of Egypt's early dynastic history.
