Kings

Djer

Pharaoh Djer was an early ruler of ancient Egypt, likely the third king of the First Dynasty, reigning around 3100 BCE. He is believed to have succeeded his father, Hor-Aha, and ruled for several decades. Djer's reign is notable for the establishment of early administrative and religious practices that would shape the development of Egyptian statehood. Archaeological evidence suggests he engaged in military campaigns, possibly in Sinai and Nubia, to secure Egypt's borders and expand its influence. Djer's tomb at Abydos is one of the largest and most elaborate of his time, indicating his significant status. Some later traditions identified his burial site with Osiris, the god of the afterlife, further emphasizing his importance in Egyptian history and mythology.
