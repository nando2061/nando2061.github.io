Kings

Userkaf

Userkaf was the founder of the Fifth Dynasty of ancient Egypt and reigned during the 25th century BCE. He is known for consolidating power and establishing a dynasty that emphasized the growing influence of the sun cult centered on the god Ra. Userkaf built the first sun temple at Abu Sir, reflecting this religious shift, while also constructing his pyramid complex at Saqqara, near the Step Pyramid of Djoser. Although his reign is believed to have been relatively brief, it marked a period of political stability and religious innovation. Historical records suggest Userkaf's rule was characterized by efforts to strengthen central authority, though details about his policies and achievements are limited. His successors continued to uphold his legacy, further advancing the prominence of solar worship in Egyptian society.
