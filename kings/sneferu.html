Kings

Sneferu

Sneferu was an ancient Egyptian pharaoh who founded the Fourth Dynasty during the Old Kingdom period, reigning around 2613–2589 BCE. Renowned for his ambitious architectural projects, he is best remembered for revolutionizing pyramid construction, marking a critical transition in Egyptian monumental architecture. Sneferu's most notable achievements include building three major pyramids: the Meidum Pyramid, which began as a step pyramid but was later modified; the Bent Pyramid at Dahshur, showcasing an unusual change in angle due to structural issues; and the Red Pyramid, which became the first successful smooth-sided pyramid, setting the standard for subsequent generations. These monumental projects reflect Sneferu's commitment to perfecting royal burial structures, as well as the immense resources and organizational skill of the Old Kingdom state under his reign. His efforts in pyramid construction also helped solidify the ideological and religious significance of these structures as symbols of divine kingship and the afterlife. Sneferu was celebrated in later traditions as a benevolent and innovative ruler, remembered as a unifier of Egypt and a patron of major advancements in architecture and statecraft. His reign was marked by stability, economic prosperity, and the expansion of trade and military campaigns, further enhancing his legacy as one of Egypt's greatest kings.
