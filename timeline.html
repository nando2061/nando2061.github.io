Timeline

c. 6000 BCE

Early agricultural communities established along the Nile River.

c. 4000 BCE

Development of trade networks and crafting of pottery and tools.

c. 3100 BCE

Narmer (or Menes) unites Upper and Lower Egypt, marking the beginning of the First Dynasty.

c. 3000 BCE

Invention of hieroglyphic writing.

c. 2900 BCE

The first royal tombs constructed at Abydos.

c. 2700 BCE

Establishment of Memphis as the capital of unified Egypt.

c. 2600 BCE

Construction of the Step Pyramid of Djoser at Saqqara by architect Imhotep.

c. 2580 BCE

Great Pyramid of Giza built under Pharaoh Khufu.

c. 2500 BCE

Construction of the Great Sphinx under Pharaoh Khafre.

c. 2200 BCE

Decline of central authority leads to the First Intermediate Period.

c. 2150 BCE

Period of drought and famine due to low Nile flooding.

c. 2100 BCE

Rise of regional rulers ("nomarchs") competing for power.

c. 2055 BCE

Mentuhotep II reunites Egypt, starting the Middle Kingdom.

c. 2000 BCE

Egyptian expansion into Nubia begins, securing valuable resources like gold.

c. 1870 BCE

Amenemhat I moves the capital to Itjtawy, near modern-day Faiyum.

c. 1850 BCE

Construction of massive irrigation systems around Faiyum.

c. 1800 BCE

First known literary work, The Tale of Sinuhe, written.

c. 1650 BCE

Hyksos invaders establish control over northern Egypt, introducing horses, chariots, and bronze weapons.

c. 1570 BCE

Kamose and Ahmose I of Thebes begin campaigns to expel the Hyksos.

c. 1550 BCE

Ahmose I reunites Egypt and founds the 18th Dynasty.

c. 1479 BCE

Ahmose I reunites Egypt and founds the 18th Dynasty.

c. 1457 BCE

Thutmose III leads the Battle of Megiddo, one of the first recorded battles in history.

c. 1353 BCE

Akhenaten introduces monotheism, worshiping Aten (the Sun Disk).

c. 1333 BCE

Tutankhamun ascends the throne; later restores polytheism.

c. 1274 BCE

Ramses II leads Egypt at the Battle of Kadesh against the Hittites, resulting in the first recorded peace treaty.

c. 1250 BCE

Construction of the great temples at Abu Simbel by Ramses II.

c. 1200 BCE

Egypt faces invasions by the "Sea Peoples."

c. 1150 BCE

Reign of Ramses III; Egypt's last great pharaoh repels the Sea Peoples.

c. 1000 BCE

Libyan pharaohs from the 22nd Dynasty control parts of Egypt.

c. 750 BCE

Nubian kings conquer Egypt, establishing the 25th Dynasty.

c. 671 BCE

Assyrians invade Egypt, reducing it to a vassal state.

c. 664 BCE

Psamtik I reunites Egypt under the Saite Dynasty (26th Dynasty).

c. 525 BCE

Persian Empire conquers Egypt under Cambyses II.

c. 404 BCE

Brief period of independence under native rulers.

c. 343 BCE

Persia reconquers Egypt.

332 BCE

Alexander the Great conquers Egypt; founds the city of Alexandria.

305 BCE

Ptolemy I Soter establishes the Ptolemaic Dynasty, blending Greek and Egyptian cultures.

196 BCE

Rosetta Stone created during the reign of Ptolemy V, key to deciphering hieroglyphics.

31 BCE

Cleopatra VII and Mark Antony defeated at the Battle of Actium by Octavian.

30 BCE

Cleopatra VII commits suicide; Egypt becomes a Roman province.

394 AD

The last known hieroglyphic inscription is carved on a wall at the Temple of Philae in southern Egypt.
