Gods

Major Deites

Osiris

God of death and resurrection who rules the underworld and enlivens vegetation, the sun god, and deceased souls

Ra

The foremost Egyptian sun god, involved in creation and the afterlife. Mythological ruler of the gods, father of every Egyptian king, and the patron god of Heliopolis.

Horus

A major god, usually shown as a falcon or as a human child, linked with the sky, the sun, kingship, protection, and healing. Often said to be the son of Osiris and Isis

Shu

Embodiment of wind or air, a member of the Ennead

Anubis

The god of funerals, embalming and protector of the dead

Sekhmet

A lioness goddess, both destructive and violent and capable of warding off disease, protector of the pharaohs who led them in war, the consort of Ptah and one of many forms of the Eye of Ra

Geb

An earth god and member of the Ennead

Isis

Wife of Osiris and mother of Horus, linked with funerary rites, motherhood, protection, and magic. She became a major deity in Greek and Roman religion

Thoth

A moon god, and a god of writing and scribes, and patron deity of Hermopolis

Nut

A sky goddess, a member of the Ennead

Atum

A creator god and solar deity, first god of the Ennead

Ptah

A creator deity and god of craftsmen, the patron god of Memphis

Set

An ambivalent god, characterized by violence, chaos, and strength, connected with the desert. Mythological murderer of Osiris and enemy of Horus, but also a supporter of the king

Ma'at

Goddess who personified truth, justice, and order

Neith

A creator and hunter goddess, patron of the city of Sais in Lower Egypt

Nepthys

A member of the Ennead, the consort of Set, who mourned Osiris alongside Isis

Amun

A creator god, patron deity of the city of Thebes, and the preeminent deity in Egypt during the New Kingdom

Tefnut

Lioness goddess of moisture and a member of the Ennead
