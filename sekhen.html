Sekhen

Pharaoh Sekhen (also known as Hor-Ka) was an early ruler of ancient Egypt, believed to have reigned during the Predynastic Period, around 3200 BCE. His existence is debated among Egyptologists, as the evidence for his reign is primarily derived from limited artifacts and inscriptions, including his serekh (a royal emblem) found at sites like Abydos. Hor-Ka is considered one of the rulers who preceded the unification of Upper and Lower Egypt. He is associated with the Thinite region in Upper Egypt, and his name, which translates to "He who embraces Horus," signifies a connection to the god Horus. His historical role, while obscure, is seen as part of the foundation of the pharaonic tradition that culminated in Egypt's early dynasties.
