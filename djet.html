Kings

Djet

Pharaoh Djet, also known as Wadj or Zet, was an early ruler of Ancient Egypt during the First Dynasty, around 3100-2890 BCE. He is believed to have reigned for a relatively short period and was likely the successor of Pharaoh Djer. Djet is primarily known from his tomb at Abydos (Tomb Z), The tomb of Djet is famous for its well-preserved stelae depicting his name and for artifacts reflecting early dynastic art and religion. His reign contributed to the consolidation of the early Egyptian state and the development of royal iconography. Djet was succeeded by Queen Merneith, who may have acted as regent for his young heir, Den.
