Kings

Den

Pharaoh Den was a ruler of Egypt during the First Dynasty (c. 2970 BCE). He is known for his effective and prosperous reign, characterized by military success and advancements in state administration. Den was one of the first pharaohs to adopt the title nswt-bity, symbolizing his rule over Upper and Lower Egypt. His reign is well-documented through inscriptions, including one of the earliest known depictions of a pharaoh wearing the double crown of Egypt. Archaeological evidence, such as his tomb in Abydos, highlights his significance and the innovation of his era, including early examples of stone architecture and the use of a staircase in a royal tomb.
